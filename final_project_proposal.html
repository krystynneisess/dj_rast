Title: DJ Rast

Summary: DJ Rast is a photo-manipulation program which reacts to audio input. Specifically, we will be mapping qualities such as frequency and amplitude patterns from MP3 files to various rasterization and SVG transformations. Through DJ Rast, we hope to explore and visualize the interrelationship between said audio and visual features.

Team Members: Krystyn Neisess, Huu Tinh Pham, Chase Porter

Background

Our idea is essentially a kind of photomanipulation. We are interested in what kind of patterns will emerge from different patterns in sound as well as whether we can get an image to respond to different sampling techniques of the sound. For example, if there is aliasing in the sampling of the sound, will there be aliasing in the image? The challenges we face are parsing image files, extracting meaningful information out of it that somehow corresponds to information we can extract or modify in an image, as well as thinking about how to essentially "paint" an image out of different materials. This involves thinking about how materials like oil pant behave and how that can be modeled as a filter over an image. We think this would be a really fun and visually interesting project that adds an additional element to computer graphics that we haven't explored yet, realtime filtering based on a signal. We think if we are successful this project would be very interesting to look at while listening to a favorite song/ looking at a favorite image.
